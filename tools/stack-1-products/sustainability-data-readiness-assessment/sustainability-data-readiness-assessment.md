## What You're Getting

A comprehensive self-assessment tool that evaluates your current capability to respond to buyer sustainability data requests. You'll discover exactly where you stand, what gaps exist, and how to close them before buyer deadlines.

**The core problem this solves:** Agricultural suppliers don't know they have sustainability data gaps until buyers send questionnaires with 2-4 week response deadlines. This assessment prevents that crisis by showing you NOW what buyers will ask for LATER—giving you time to build capability before requests arrive.

### 1. Buyer Requirement Readiness Scorecard (Excel)

**Comprehensive evaluation tool** assessing your capability across 12 common buyer sustainability requirements.

**Assessment categories:**

**Carbon Footprint Data**
- Scope 1 emissions (direct operational emissions)
- Scope 2 emissions (purchased electricity/energy)
- Scope 3 upstream (purchased goods, transportation)
- Can you provide: ☐ No data ☐ Estimates ☐ Partial data ☐ Complete data ☐ Verified data
- Methodology documented? Supporting evidence available?
- Ready to report within 2 weeks?

**Energy Consumption**
- Total energy use by source (electricity, fuel types)
- Renewable vs. non-renewable breakdown
- Energy intensity metrics (per unit output)
- Historical trend data (year-over-year comparison)
- Reduction targets and progress tracking

**Water Use**
- Source tracking (municipal, groundwater, surface, rainwater)
- Process-level consumption data
- Water intensity metrics
- Seasonal variation documentation
- Water stress risk assessment (location-based)

**Waste Management**
- Total waste generation by stream (organic, recyclable, hazardous, landfill)
- Diversion rates (% recycled/composted)
- Waste reduction initiatives and results
- Hazardous waste compliance documentation

**Sustainable Sourcing Practices**
- Certified input usage (organic, sustainable, verified)
- Supply chain traceability (knowing origin of key inputs)
- Supplier sustainability requirements
- Deforestation-free commitments (where relevant)

**Biodiversity & Land Use**
- Total operational land area breakdown
- Conservation area allocation (buffer zones, wildlife habitat)
- Biodiversity protection measures
- Ecologically sensitive area management
- Soil health monitoring and improvement practices

**Chemical Use & Management**
- Pesticide/herbicide application tracking (products, rates, locations)
- Integrated pest management practices
- Chemical storage and handling compliance
- Reduction plans and progress

**Animal Welfare (Livestock Operations)**
- Housing and living conditions documentation
- Health and veterinary care protocols
- Antibiotic use policy and tracking
- Welfare certification status

**Labor & Social Practices**
- Fair labor compliance (wages, hours, conditions)
- Health and safety programs and incident tracking
- Worker training documentation
- Social compliance certifications

**Climate Adaptation & Resilience**
- Climate risk assessment (what threatens your operation?)
- Adaptation measures implemented
- Business continuity planning
- Yield/productivity stability under climate stress

**Certifications & Standards**
- Current certifications held (organic, sustainability, quality)
- Audit history and compliance track record
- Standards alignment (even if not certified)
- Certification roadmap and timeline

**Sustainability Governance**
- Sustainability policy documentation
- Responsible party assignment
- Performance review frequency
- Improvement target setting and tracking
- Stakeholder engagement practices

**Scoring methodology:**
- Each category scored 0-5 (No capability → Verified/Exemplary capability)
- Weighted by buyer priority (carbon/energy/water typically highest)
- Generates overall readiness score (0-100%)
- Identifies critical gaps (categories scoring <2)
- Highlights quick wins (categories near completion with small effort)

**Output:**
- Overall readiness percentage
- Category-by-category breakdown with color coding (red/yellow/green)
- Critical gap identification
- Quick win opportunities
- Comparison to "supplier best practice" benchmarks

**Delivery format:**
- Excel scorecard with automated scoring and visualization
- Instructions for completing each assessment category
- Benchmark data showing what "good" looks like

### 2. Gap Analysis & Prioritization Matrix (Excel)

**Strategic planning tool** that takes your scorecard results and generates a prioritized action roadmap.

**How it works:**

**Input your scorecard results** → Automatically maps each gap against:

**Stakeholder Priority**
- Which requirements matter most to buyers in your sector?
- Agricultural buyers typically prioritize: carbon data > water use > sustainable sourcing > certifications
- Processing/manufacturing buyers add: waste management, chemical use
- Retail buyers emphasize: certifications, traceability, social practices

**Implementation Difficulty**
- Data availability (do you generate this info already vs. need new systems?)
- Technical complexity (simple tracking vs. complex calculation?)
- Resource requirements (time, people, money)
- Lead time (quick fixes vs. multi-month builds)

**Cost to Close Gap**
- Zero cost (better documentation of existing practices)
- Low cost (€0-500): simple templates, basic tracking improvements
- Medium cost (€500-2,000): sensors, software subscriptions, consultant hours
- High cost (€2,000+): certification audits, major system builds, third-party verification

**Risk Level**
- Critical: Inability to respond threatens supplier status
- High: Gaps that damage credibility or limit growth opportunities
- Medium: Improvements that strengthen position but aren't immediately urgent
- Low: Nice-to-have enhancements with limited stakeholder impact

**The matrix generates:**

**Quadrant 1 - Critical Quick Wins (High Priority, Low Difficulty)**
- Close these gaps FIRST - maximum impact, minimum effort
- Example: "Document existing composting practices" vs. "Build composting from scratch"
- Typical timeline: 2-6 weeks

**Quadrant 2 - Strategic Priorities (High Priority, High Difficulty)**
- Critical gaps requiring significant effort
- Need project planning and resource allocation
- Often multi-month builds (carbon accounting, certification pursuit)
- Start planning now even if implementation is 6-12 months out

**Quadrant 3 - Opportunistic Improvements (Low Priority, Low Difficulty)**
- Easy wins with limited stakeholder impact
- Do these when bandwidth allows, but don't stress them
- Often "documentation theater" rather than operational change

**Quadrant 4 - Deprioritize (Low Priority, High Difficulty)**
- Gaps that don't matter enough to justify the effort
- Examples: Niche certifications buyers don't ask for, excessive detail on minor metrics
- Maybe revisit in year 2-3, but ignore for now

**Output:**
- Visual quadrant matrix plotting all your gaps
- Prioritized action list (what to tackle in what order)
- Resource requirement summary (time, cost, expertise needed)
- Timeline recommendation (immediate vs. short-term vs. long-term)

**Delivery format:**
- Excel tool with automated quadrant plotting
- Action roadmap template (editable)
- Resource planning worksheet
