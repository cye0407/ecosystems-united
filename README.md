## Philosophy

### Sustainability: Two Meanings

**Environmental sustainability** — reducing emissions, managing resources responsibly, meeting regulatory requirements (CSRD, Scope 3, ecolabels) — is real and it matters. We don't redefine it. We operationalize the path to it.

**Operational sustainability** — the capacity to adapt, to match strategy to moment, to survive and thrive as conditions change — is what the Five Stacks builds.

The relationship:

| Five Stacks builds... | Which enables... |
|----------------------|------------------|
| Metrics (Stack 1) | Data foundation for Scope 3, CSRD disclosure |
| Efficiency (Stack 2) | Actual emissions and resource reduction |
| Circularity (Stack 3) | Waste reduction, circular economy credentials |
| Resilience (Stack 4) | Climate adaptation, supply chain robustness |
| Regenerative (Stack 5) | Ecolabels, certifications, credible claims |

**Environmental sustainability is the proof. The stacks are how you earn it.**

### Adaptivity

Operational sustainability isn't a static state. It's not a target or a certification.

**It's adaptivity.**

The capacity to respond appropriately to context. Knowing when to be efficient, when to go all-in, when to dig in, when to pivot. Like nature: no steady state, just continuous adjustment to conditions.

Survival of the fittest — and the fittest aren't the strongest. They're the ones who fit their environment.

### Operational Efficacy (Not Just Efficiency)

- **Efficiency** = do more with less (short-term, narrow)
- **Efficacy** = do the right thing at the right time with a future state in mind (contextual, adaptive)

Efficacy rejects the myth of the "rational actor" optimizing for a static world. It embraces bounded knowledge, changing conditions, and the reality that context determines what "right" means.

### Competitive Advantage

The outcome of the Five Stacks isn't just "sustainability" — it's compounding competitive advantage:

- Lower costs, higher margins
- Defensible positioning
- Resilience to shocks AND shifts
- The ability to adapt as conditions change

These aren't separate outcomes. They're the same capability viewed through different lenses.

## The Five Stacks Framework

### Structure

**Stack 0: Fundamentals** (the frame)

Before you optimize anything, establish what's true:
- What business are you in?
- What environment are you operating in?
- What are your constraints (budget, resources, capacity)?
- What's your goal (growth, stability, exit, legacy)?

This is the orientation. Revisited during planning cycles, major changes, or when something feels off.

**Stacks 1-5: The Capability Progression**

Each stack has three dimensions:

| Dimension | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| **Internal** | What's happening inside your operation? |
| **External** | What's happening in your environment? |
| **Financial** | What can you afford? What does it cost? What's the return? |

### The Stacks

| Stack | Name | Core Question | Internal | External | Financial |
|-------|------|---------------|----------|----------|-----------|
| **0** | Fundamentals | What's true before we begin? | Your constraints, capabilities | Your landscape, game you're playing | Budget reality, resource limits |
| **1** | Metrics | Do we see clearly? | What are we doing? | What game are we playing? | What's our budget reality? |
| **2** | Efficiency | Are we leaking value? | Where are we bleeding margin? | Where is the market leaking? (opportunity) | What's the ROI on fixing it? |
| **3** | Circularity | Are we capturing value? | What value are we missing? | What value is the ecosystem missing? | What's it worth to capture? |
| **4** | Resilience | Can we absorb shocks? | What could break us? | What's shifting around us? | What's the cost of fragility vs. redundancy? |
| **5** | Regenerative | Are we compounding advantage? | How do we position? | Where is the landscape going? | What premium can we capture? |

### Principles

| Stack | Principle |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 - Fundamentals | Know what's true before you optimize |
| 1 - Metrics | If you can't see it, you can't improve it |
| 2 - Efficiency | Fix leaks before adding initiatives |
| 3 - Circularity | Every output can be someone's input |
| 4 - Resilience | Systems must flex, not just endure |
| 5 - Regenerative | Advantage compounds when it's built into operations |

## The Product

### What It Is

Five Stacks is an **organizational operating system**, not a one-time assessment.

| One-time tool | Operating system |
|---------------|------------------|
| Do it once, get a report | Live with it, update it, run your business through it |
| Static snapshot | Dynamic, evolving picture |
| "Here's what's wrong" | "Here's where you are, here's where you're going, here's what changed" |
| Completion = done | Completion = baseline established, now you operate |

### Rhythm

| Trigger | What happens |
|---------|--------------|
| Initial setup | Full run through Fundamentals + Stacks 1-5, establish baseline |
| Planning cycles | Revisit relevant stacks, update context, set priorities |
| Growth / change | Re-run affected stacks |
| Alerts / reminders | System nudges when things drift or go stale |
| Review rhythm | User-defined (quarterly, annually, continuous) |

### Relationship to Other Systems

Five Stacks **complements** existing planning systems (OKRs, EOS, annual planning).

- OKRs tell you what you're trying to achieve
- EOS gives you meeting rhythms and accountability
- **Five Stacks does the groundwork** — the visibility, the capability assessment, the internal/external/financial clarity that those systems assume you have

It's the capability layer underneath execution systems.

### Voice & Role

The system is a **Navigator-Facilitator with embedded coaching**.

| Role | How it shows up |
|------|-----------------|
| Navigator | Shows you where you are, what's around you, what paths exist |
| Facilitator | Structures the journey, connects the dots, keeps you moving |
| Coach (embedded) | Celebrates progress, prompts reflection, normalizes setbacks — through the experience, not lectures |

### What It Does

| Function | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Illuminates | "Your energy costs are 40% above industry benchmark" |
| Reflects | "You said your goal was margin improvement. Your Stack 2 score hasn't moved in 6 months." |
| Surfaces gaps | "You have strong internal metrics but haven't updated external landscape since Q1" |
| Provides context | "Companies in your vertical are facing X regulatory shift" |
| Shares patterns | "Organizations with similar profiles often prioritize Y" |
| Prompts questions | "Have you considered...?" / "What would change if...?" |

### What It Does NOT Do

| Function | Why not |
|----------|---------|
| Prescribe actions | "You should do X" — No. You decide. |
| Promise outcomes | "This will increase profit by Y%" — No. We don't control your execution. |
| Replace judgment | You lead. The system informs. |
| Take responsibility | This is your business. We give you clarity. You own the outcomes. |

> Five Stacks is a mirror, not a consultant. We illuminate. You decide.
